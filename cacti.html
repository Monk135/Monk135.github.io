Cacti

5v5 Deathmatch Map for Call of Duty: Black Ops 2

Unreal Engine 5.5 FPS Multiplayer Kevin Lynch Theory 2 Weeks

Overview

Type

5v5 Deathmatch Map

Engine

Unreal Engine 5.5

Duration

2 Weeks

Setting

American South-West

Cacti is a FPS multiplayer map designed for Call of Duty: Black Ops 2's deathmatch mode. The primary research focus was American South-West architecture — studying references, identifying stylistic patterns, and translating those into a functional level design kit before building the layout.

Design Approach

The layout was structured using Kevin Lynch's urban theory — organizing the space into Edges (walls and boundaries), Zones (distinct areas with thematic identity), Nodes (key decision points), and Lines (primary movement corridors and clash points).

The map is intentionally asymmetric with a traversable central building, offering players meaningful choices between open engagement and close-quarters combat.

Lynch Theory Diagram — Edges, Zones, Clashpoints

Pre-Production

Research began with architectural reference gathering and a Miro board analysis of South-West building similarities. A Level Design Kit (LDK) and demo were built based on CoD:BO2 metrics before any layout work began.

Design Techniques

Landmarks (Macro & Micro): Large landmarks visible from multiple positions aid mental mapping and orientation. Smaller micro-landmarks support callouts and make zones feel distinct and memorable.

Landmark Screenshots

Verticality: Multiple elevations across the map create dynamic navigation, interesting lines of sight, and force players to aim at varying heights — increasing combat complexity without overcomplicating the layout.

Verticality Screenshots

Player Agency & Balance: The map offers both closed, tight corridors and open engagement zones. Some players prefer sniping and open sightlines; others prefer close-quarters brawls. The layout accommodates both without either dominating.

At any given decision point, players face no more than 3–4 paths simultaneously — keeping choices meaningful without creating cognitive overload.

Reflections

Challenge

Lines of Sight

Long sightlines through small gaps created balance issues. Future solution: integrate bubble diagrams into planning to identify zone access and LoS problems earlier in the process.

Challenge

Readability

Walls and ground were too visually similar, making gameplay zones hard to distinguish. Solution: darker ground materials and increased complexity in non-gameplay spaces to differentiate them.

Challenge

Zone Variety

Zones lacked sufficient differentiation. Addressed through varied shapes, sizes, and lighting, plus thematic chunking per zone to aid spatial mapping.

Challenge

Playtesting

The level was built in a blank Unreal project with no gameplay scripting — making it impossible to properly test sight lines or game feel. Next step: use game templates with AI or player support for future projects.
