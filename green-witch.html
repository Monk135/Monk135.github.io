Green Witch

Weekend game jam entry — foraging, feeding, and dark comedy

Game Jam Top-Down Weekend Project
Play on Itch.io ↗
Green Witch — Screenshot

Overview

Context

Gameable Mentorship Program Ice-Breaker

Theme

World Hunger

Duration

Weekend (~12 hours)

Green Witch was made as an ice-breaking game jam for mentors of the Gameable program — an initiative teaching young girls how to make video games. The theme was world hunger, explored through a darkly comedic lens.

You play as a witch who forages food from trash cans to feed children a varied diet — before eating them. A healthy child makes for a healthy witch.

How It Works

Players begin with a 2-minute timer. Find a child, learn their dietary needs, then scavenge the right food groups from nearby trash cans. Glowing pillars indicate regions more likely to hold specific food types. Each trash can triggers a claw machine mini-game — with a chance of a raccoon jumpscare. Feed the child a varied diet, then prepare for the next level with more children and tighter time constraints.

Reflections

Working within a tight 12-hour limit taught me how to prioritize core gameplay features and scope realistically under pressure. It confirmed the importance of clear communication, quick iteration, and trusting others' expertise to move a project forward efficiently — all within a team of people I had just met.
