Puzzle Design · Pacing · Narrative Arc

Curyeux is a published third-person puzzle-platformer. I designed the final level — a slow-paced, introspective experience built around mastering mobile platforms and culminating in a cinematic meeting with the player character's inner child.

As Lead Assist, I also handled interdepartmental communication, coordinating between design, art, and programming teams throughout production.

Design Intentions

The level was built around three pillars: mastering the mobile platforms introduced earlier in the game, a slow and cozy pace suited to the game's introspective tone, and a narrative arc that leads to an emotional payoff — meeting the player's inner child.

Three puzzle sections of increasing difficulty are each preceded by a narrative event, building emotional momentum toward the final cinematic.

Production

The level began with research, a 2D plan, and a full Level Design Document. Construction followed the plan with regular playtests and feedback loops.

Initial Blockout — GIF

Problem: Mobile platforms discouraged exploration — players ignored them and moved on.
Solution: Replaced with stairs and widened the space.
Result: Improved exploration flow, better pacing, and more creative freedom for the art team.

Platform Iteration — GIF

Problem: Players couldn't find the platforms — readability was low.
Solution: Added verticality, centralized platforms within camera framing.
Result: Transformed an observation puzzle into a physical navigation challenge with much better readability.

Blueprints & Scripting

Level logic was implemented in Unreal Blueprints. Key interactions included proximity-triggered platform appearances and disappearing platforms on player exit — both reinforcing the puzzle design intent.

Reflections

Challenge

Coherence

Lack of rhythm and balance consistency across levels. Resolved by creating shared design pillar documentation with mechanical complexity targets per level.

Challenge

Guidance

Players got lost despite clear visual cues. Solution: layered audio cues, adjusted lighting, and geometric leading lines built into the architecture.

Challenge

Time Constraints

Short deadlines limited iteration cycles. Adapted by building core player flow first, adding complexity later, and using modular design for swappable sections.

Challenge

Scoping

Level length exceeded a single artist's capacity. Solution: multi-purpose spaces using vertical layering, and expandable zones that start small but can be widened.
