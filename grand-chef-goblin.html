Grand Chef Goblin

Non-linear text-based adventure

Twine Narrative Design Non-Linear Branch & Bottleneck
Play on Itch.io ↗
Grand Chef Goblin — Screenshot

Overview

Tool

Twine

Context

Narrative Design Course — Campus ADN

Inspiration

Reigns

Grand Chef Goblin is a non-linear text-based mini-game built in Twine for a narrative design course. The player takes the role of a goblin tribe chief, navigating a series of absurd encounters while balancing four stats — inspired by the card-swipe mechanics of Reigns.

Design Technique

The structure uses a branch & bottleneck approach with reused variables. Player choices diverge meaningfully across branches, but narrative threads converge back at key bottleneck moments — allowing maximum player impact while keeping the scope manageable for a solo project.

Flowcharts were planned in Draw.io before any Twine coding began, which helped identify structural problems early and prevented scope creep.

Reflections

This project demonstrated how quickly non-linear scope can spiral — even at mini-game scale. The initial flowchart wasn't enough; a second iteration was needed before the structure felt workable. The lesson: prototype the narrative structure before writing any content, and scope conservatively for branching stories.
